StartUp Purdue

  • kkamiya
  • May 7, 2019
  • Uncategorized
  • 0 Comments

In this event students were expected to propose technology-based solutions to problems faced by the general Purdue University public. The ideas each of the teams developed would be pitched to a group of experienced investors who will decide the value and applicability of each idea.

I was placed into a team of four and we immediately started to work. Our idea was to create a localizing dining apps where students will be able to order their meals and go pick them up. Once the idea was formulated, we began to do market research. By understanding the consumerâ€™s tastes and desires it will become simpler to understand what type of product we must create. Meanwhile I conducted financial research to understand how long it would take to make profit. The financial analytics gives key investors a method to understand the long-term logistics of the project. All of this information was compiled into a easy to follow start-up style presentation.

The experience was fantastic for me. What drew me to this competition was the entrepreneurial topic. Whereas a standard case competition required students to evaluate a firm as an outsider, the startup challenge instead forced you to take a new perspective. Being forced to analyze business problems from such a unique perspective required a different level of thinking that is rare to see. Just being part of this has made me more interested in the business world and the potential to start my own business.
