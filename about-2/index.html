I'm Kenzo Kamiya

I am Kenzo Kamiya, a Japanese Mechanical Engineering student in Purdue University. As a 21-year-old Japanese student I have had a life time of experience developing my skills as a global citizen.

My strength as an individual comes from my diverse international background. By the time I had entered Purdue University I have lived in Japan, Venezuela, United States, Peru, and Kenya. Being able to live such a multiethnic early life has been a great privilege that I am forever thankful for. It has given me the opportunity to expand my interests into cultures and understand how to interact with those different from me. Networking has never been easier. Moreover, it has empowered me to face the issues facing the globalized society we live in today.

Much like my upbringing, my interests are diverse. I find myself jumping from one exercise to another learning something new through the process. I believe that having a wide and strong foundation gives me the unique opportunity to develop myself in any direction I please. By touching bases with multiple different disciplines like economics, computer science, and mechanical engineering, I see to discover my interests and improve myself as an individual.

I hope you have gotten a better idea as to what kind of person I am. I am not just any mechanical engineer. I am a global citizen. I am a Pioneer. I am passionate, but most of all I am Kenzo Kamiya.
