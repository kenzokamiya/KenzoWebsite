DAAD RISE GERMANY

Dortmund University of Technology, Dortmund, Germany, May 2019 — August 2019

The title of the project is manufacturing of load optimized automotive components by incremental sheet-bulk metal forming. Researchers will be expected to perform experimental studies on incremental sheet bulk mental forming operations to analyze the influence of different process parameters and tool designs on the achievable properties and accuracies of the components. The studies will include the experiments along with investigations such as metallurgical analysis to determine the geometry and cold hardening achieved.

UNDERGRADUATE RESEARCH ASSISTANT

Intelligent Infrastructure Systems Laboratory (Purdue University), IN, May 2018 – Present

Part of the team lead by Professor Shirley Dyke aiming to develop a camera vision based deep learning algorithm to analyze structural damage caused to buildings. By using images taken of the damage caused by hurricanes, we aim to develop a program that would be able to remove the need for human presence in damage evaluation.
My responsibilities during this time are predominantly organizing and manipulating a large database of images detailing the effects of hurricanes. By organizing the information, the computer analytics program can properly identify the desired outcome. Moreover, I feed the computer useable information using software to mark out and identify objects for the computer vision software.

ECONOMIC ANALYST INTERN

International Monetary Fund, Nairobi, Kenya, June 2017 — August 2017

As an intern within the International Monetary Fund I was required to collect and analyze data extensively to understand how the Kenyan economy reacts to elections. Reviewing official government documents and evaluating the value and accuracy of data was expected. The internship resulted in the creation of an excel document detailing the trends of the Kenyan economy quarterly from the year of 2004 to 2016, a presentation on the topic, and a written memo to be sent to the IMF headquarters. Two of the key processes of research, data acquisition and processing, are both skills that I have gained through this internship.

DELOITTE STARTUP PURDUE INNOVATION COMPETITION

Purdue University, West Lafayette IN, April 2019

Within this competition hosted by Deloitte students were expected to develop technology-related improvement, solution, or new opportunity for Purdue University. DELOITTE CYBER THREAT COMPETITION

Deloitte University, Houston, TX, February 2019

2nd place

Participated in an intercollegiate competition hosted by the Deloitte Foundation. The objective of the competition is to solve practical cyber security challenges in a simulated environment. Broken into two rounds, selected participants must first compete within their respective university to qualify for the final round. This first round consists of an examination of one’s security acumen and numerous online challenges. The winners of round 1 are selected to form a four-man team. This team travels to Texas to participate in the real-time cyber security breach simulation in competition with 15 other universities.

ENTREPRENEURSHIP DESIGN TEAM

Purdue University, Houston, TX, February 2019

In this entrepreneurship team three mechanical engineers were put together to go through the engineering design process. The goal of our team was to design an attachment that would help increase mobility of wheelchair users when it snowed and ultimately bring it to the market. To make our idea into reality we had to combine MATLAB, Excel, and CATIA to move through each stage of the design process. Finally, we manufactured our product using various machining techniques. Through this process I learned more about the importance of not only having a proficiency in the technical, but also having the mindset to communicate and collaborate effectively.

EPICS AAEE Team

Purdue University, West Lafayette, IN, Fall 2019

The Aero and Astro Engineering Education (AAEE) team aims to develop activities for young students to become interested in STEM education. While the team worked on multiple projects and a time, I worked on the robotic arm team. The goal of this project was to manufacture a robotic arm that mimicked the overall behavior of the Shuttle Remort Manipulator System (SRMS) on the International Space Station. I worked on the manufacturing and assembly of the arms. The result of our team’s work was presented to the Purdue hosted event Space Day. Here children would be able to play with the arms and learn about the International Space Station and the engineering necessary to make such an endeavor possible.

EPICS NDP Team

Purdue University, West Lafayette, IN, Summer 2018

EPICS gave me the unique opportunity to collaborate with people from differing disciplines to develop community-based projects. I was part of the Natural Disaster Preparedness (NDP) team and our objective was to develop a device capable of refrigerating insulin over a period of three months. Success for such an ambitious project could only be achieved by taking advantage of the varied majors of each member. As a mechanical engineer, I focused on the design and thermodynamics of the product. Using constant feedback from other members, I developed two unique CAD based models and a prototype for testing. At the end of the development period we presented our progress to industry members. This experience improved my capacity to work within a team composed of people from varied backgrounds. By acting as the design manager and going through the engineering design process I experienced what is necessary to develop an effective team.

ELECTRIC SKATEBOARD DESIGN

Purdue University, West Lafayette, IN, Fall 2019

Building an electric skateboard was something that I have been planning to do for a couple of months. It was only when I got the support of a close friend in electrical engineering, that I decided to go through with it. To ensure success, I applied the design methodology I learned from my ME entrepreneurship course. I went through three phases: problem definition, concept generation, and product design. Before I could begin to design the product, I conducted several performance analyses. Using this information, I chose a dual engine set up driven by a belt drive. Afterwards, I designed the motor mount and created the envisioned product in SolidWorks. Once I completed the project, I posted both the engineering process taken and the CAD model online for the benefit of others. Being able to develop the project was a fulfilling experience, one that reinforced the knowledge base I gained from my classes.

DELOITTE CASE COMPETITION

Purdue University, West Lafayette, IN, Spring 2018

In my sophomore year I took part in the Deloitte case competition to do a close friend a favor. At the end of the experience I had discovered a potential career path. In the case competition our team of four was tasked to provide business solutions to a fictional car manufacturing firm. In order to provide an appropriate response, all aspects of the situation had to be evaluated. This includes work force composition, revenue, corporate structure, and desired performance. At the end of the process our work was presented to a panel of Deloitte members to be judged for quality. The active and dynamic setting that required teams to combine communication, problem solving, and active learning skills was enjoyable. Ever since then I’ve wanted to become more involved in the area of consulting.
